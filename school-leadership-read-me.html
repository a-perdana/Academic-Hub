1 Welcome

This is the School Leaders Workspace for your partner school. It's where the leadership team — Foundation Reps, the School Principal, Academic + Cambridge Coordinators — runs the operational week together.

It's the school-scoped counterpart to the HQ Coordinators workspace at Central Hub. Same shape, same discipline; just narrowed to the people at this school instead of the whole network.

🔒
Same-school visibility. Every row you create here carries a schoolId. Only the leadership team at this school can read it. Other partner schools cannot. HQ admin can — but that's an audit path, not a daily one.

2 The flow

The 7 pages in this workspace tell one story: meetings produce decisions, decisions become activities, activities anchor against artifacts. The directory keeps track of who's in the room.

  1. Meetings capture what was discussed. Each agenda item can be tagged decision / action / discussion.
  2. Decisions surface the binding rules from those meetings. Auditable across academic years; supersede rather than edit.
  3. Activities capture the kanban work that flows out of those decisions. Action-tagged items spawn cards here.
  4. Artifacts are the living documents (Annual Plan, SoW, intervention plan) the decisions and activities point to.
  5. Directory tells everyone who owns what — the people side of the workspace.
  6. My Observations + Team Induction + Weekly Checklist are personal lenses on the same school context.

3 Each page explained

Read Me First (you are here)

5-minute orientation. Read once, then come back when you forget which page does what.

Operational Guide

First-90-days handbooks for the four sub-roles. Tells you why your role exists and how to ramp. Pairs with Weekly Checklist (which tells you what to do this week).

Meetings

Leadership team meeting minutes. Each meeting carries agenda items; items can be tagged decision / action / discussion / announcement / red-text. Red-text items promote into Decisions; action items spawn cards in Activities.

Decisions

The auditable register of binding school-level rules — "the dress code is X", "Year 7-8 observation feedback rooms have a third person present". Status: active / draft / superseded. Supersede rather than edit, so the trail stays intact.

Directory

The leadership team at this school. Curated — not auto-synced from the user database — so departures and transitions can be reflected immediately. Search by name / subject / role.

Artifacts (School Resources)

Per-subject living documents — Annual Plan, Scheme of Work, lesson plans, internal assessments, intervention plans. Each subject card collects what's current; superseded versions stay archived.

Activities

School-scoped kanban. Mirrors the network-wide /activities surface but constrained to this school. Action-tagged meeting items can spawn cards here automatically; you can also create direct projects + tasks.

My Observations

Personal lens — formal appraisals + walkthroughs you've submitted as a leader. Empty on day 1; observations land here automatically after you submit them from the Teacher Appraisal / Walkthrough Entry pages.

Weekly Checklist

Personal lens — your own operational rhythm for this week, scoped to your active sub-role. Pairs with the Operational Guide handbook for your role.

Team Induction

The first-year inductions at your school. As school principal you have read access to the team's progress, observation cycles, and pulse roll-up. Charter NN2 applies: aggregate signals visible, named journal entries are not.

4 Your first week

  1. Day 1. Open the Operational Guide and read the handbook for your sub-role (foundation rep / principal / academic coord / cambridge coord). Skim the 4-stage timeline.
  2. Day 2. Open the Directory. Add yourself if not present. Glance at the other leaders.
  3. Day 3. Open Meetings. Find the most recent minute. Read the agenda items — context for what's live.
  4. Day 4. Open Decisions. Filter by Active. These are the binding rules at this school. Know them.
  5. Day 5. Open Artifacts. Check that the Annual Plan + SoW links work for the subjects in scope.
  6. Ongoing. Friday afternoon → open Weekly Checklist, tick what's done, leave the rest for next week.

5 Notes by sub-role

Foundation Representative

You're the owner's voice. Decisions tagged governance typically go through you. You run the cycle of school appraisals (separate suite, lives in Admin) — but the leadership decisions register is where you confirm year-on-year direction.

School Principal

You chair Meetings. Most binding Decisions originate from your meetings. Your My Observations page is where calibration anchors. Team Induction is your low-pulse early warning system — Charter NN2 says watch pulses, not journals.

Academic Coordinator

You own pacing + scheme-of-work coverage. Artifacts is your primary surface — keep the Annual Plan + SoW links current. Activities kanban tracks the curriculum delivery work.

Cambridge Coordinator

You're the Cambridge International liaison. Cambridge-tagged decisions (entries / pathway / QA) sit alongside the broader leadership register. HQ Subject Specialists visit your school 1-2 times per year — their walkthrough findings surface in My Observations.

7 Glossary

CSLS
Cambridge School Leader Standards 2023. The 6-domain × 25-competency framework that anchors leadership development. Visible as mor chips throughout the hub.
ES
Eduversal Academic Standards — the 23-section, 393-madde network policy manual. Cyan chips. Verbatim source in /references.
AICF
AI Competency Framework v1.0. Orange chips. Sits alongside CSLS/ES as a 3rd reference family for AI literacy.
schoolId
Your school's identifier in Firestore. Every row this workspace creates carries it; the rule layer uses it to enforce same-school visibility.
Sub-role
Your specific leadership lane: foundation_representative, school_principal, academic_coordinator, or cambridge_coordinator. You can hold more than one.
Supersede
When a Decision changes, you create a NEW row that supersedes the old one — the old stays visible (marked superseded). Audit trail discipline: never edit a published decision in place.
NN2
Charter Non-Negotiable 2: HQ never reads named induction journal entries. Aggregate signals (pulse, low-pulse alarm) are read; individual entries are private.
Action vs Decision
A meeting item tagged "action" spawns a kanban card in Activities. A meeting item tagged "decision" (or red-text) promotes into the binding Decisions register.

8 Stuck?

If something doesn't behave the way this page describes, you have three options:

  1. Check the Operational Guide — your sub-role handbook often answers the "who owns this" question.
  2. Send feedback — there's a feedback button on every page. Choose "Bug" or "Idea" and explain what you expected vs what happened.
  3. Email tech@eduversal.org — for blocking issues (can't sign in, page won't load, data missing). HQ tech team responds within 1 business day.
⚠
Don't share screenshots of student data in feedback. If a bug involves student records, describe the structure of the issue ("attempt N for student M in class X shows wrong score band") without pasting names or IDs. The feedback collection is admin-readable.
