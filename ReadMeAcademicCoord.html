As an Academic Coordinator, you have a group of pages in the Admin → For Academic Coordinator menu. They are the tools you use to evaluate and grow your teachers — appraisal, walkthroughs, KPI evaluation, and calibration — plus the Competency Framework that anchors it all. This page explains each one in plain English. New words are in Section 7 — Glossary.

A short promise
You do not need to learn all of this today. These tools all point at the same goal: fair, consistent, helpful feedback for teachers. Read this once so you know which tool to reach for, then use them across the year.
🧑‍🏫
What does an Academic Coordinator do here?
You sit close to teaching and learning at your school. In Academic Hub, you formally appraise teachers, do quick walkthroughs, evaluate their KPIs, and keep your scoring calibrated with other appraisers so it is fair.
🎯
Why these pages matter
Teachers grow fastest with clear, fair feedback. These pages turn classroom visits and observations into a consistent record — anchored to the same Cambridge Teacher Standards across the whole network, so a "good" at your school means the same as at any other.
Where to find these pages. They live under Admin → For Academic Coordinator in the navbar. You see this menu because you hold the Academic Coordinator role. Some pages are shared with School Principals and Cambridge Coordinators — that is by design. If a page redirects you with ?denied=..., that is a role gate; tell Eduversal.

How they fit together. Walkthroughs are the frequent, low-stakes visits. The appraisal is the formal judgement. KPI evaluation checks the agreed targets. Calibration keeps all your scoring fair and consistent with other appraisers. The Competency Framework sits underneath all of them as the shared definition of "good teaching".

Each card tells you four things: what the page is, why we built it, what you will see, and what to do.

Teacher Appraisal Entry
/teacher-appraisal-entry
Open page →
What it is
The formal teacher appraisal form. You score a teacher against the network appraisal framework (the F-items), with each item anchored to a Cambridge Teacher Standard.
Why we built it
Every teacher deserves one fair, written appraisal built on the same framework across the network. The form shows the Cambridge reference + the reason behind each item, so scoring is grounded, not guesswork. Source: appraisal-framework-v2.json.
What you see
A scoring form with rubric items, mor CTS X.Y chips you can click for the source text, and narrative boxes.
What to do
Use your walkthrough notes as evidence first, then complete the appraisal. Open each rubric item's detail to read the Cambridge anchor before you score. Shared with School Principal + Cambridge Coordinator.
👁️
Teacher Walkthrough
/teacher-walkthrough-entry
Open page →
What it is
A short classroom-visit note. Walkthroughs are quick (a few minutes), frequent, and lower-stakes than a full appraisal. You jot what you saw.
Why we built it
A single yearly appraisal is a thin picture. Frequent walkthroughs build a rich, fair record over time — and let you give teachers fast, kind feedback while it still matters.
What you see
A light form for the visit — focus, quick observations, and a note. Records show in My Observations.
What to do
Do these little and often. Aim for several across a term, not one big push. The notes become your evidence base for the formal appraisal. Shared with School Principal + Cambridge Coordinator.
📊
Teacher KPI Evaluation
/teacher-kpi-evaluation
Open page →
What it is
The evaluator dashboard for teacher KPI submissions. Teachers submit their own KPI evidence; here you review it and confirm or adjust the scores.
Why we built it
KPIs only mean something when a second person checks the evidence. This page is where you do that — fairly and on the record — for your school's teachers only.
What you see
A list of your school's teacher KPI submissions waiting for review, with status fields you flip as you evaluate. Filtered to your school.
What to do
Work through submissions on the agreed cadence. Read the teacher's evidence, then confirm or adjust. The KPI items carry CTS chips too — click for the Cambridge basis. Shared with School Principal.
⚖️
Appraisal Calibration
/appraiser-calibration
Open page →
What it is
A tool to compare your scoring with other appraisers. It surfaces where you tend to score higher or lower than your peers, so you can adjust toward a shared standard.
Why we built it
Two fair people can still score differently. Calibration is how a network keeps "a 4" meaning the same everywhere — it protects teachers from luck-of-the-appraiser. This is inter-rater reliability in plain terms.
What you see
Comparison views of your scoring patterns against the calibrated standard / other appraisers.
What to do
Visit it regularly, especially during appraisal season. If you see you drift high or low on certain items, talk it through with your team and adjust. Shared with School Principal.
🌟
Competency Framework
/competency-framework
Open page →
What it is
The shared map of leadership competencies — what good leadership looks like, grouped into 6 domains, each with 4 growth stages. The same framework everyone in Academic Hub grows against.
Why we built it
So "good" is defined once, clearly, and shared. It anchors appraisal and your own development to the Cambridge School Leader Standards 2023 plus Eduversal Academic Standards and AI competencies — hover any chip to read the source.
What you see
A browsable framework of 6 domains × competencies, each with 4-stage descriptors and a progress view. Read-only reference + your own progress.
What to do
Browse it to ground your appraisal language. Use it as the shared vocabulary when you give teachers feedback. Open to all leadership sub-roles.

This is a soft guide, not a fixed rule. It shows the natural order across one school year.

1
All year — do walkthroughs, little and often
Keep visiting classrooms with Teacher Walkthrough. A few short notes each week beat one long observation at year end.
2
Before you appraise — calibrate
Open Appraisal Calibration and check you are scoring in line with peers. Adjust before, not after, you write the formal appraisals.
3
Appraisal season — write the formal appraisal
With your walkthrough notes in hand, complete Teacher Appraisal Entry for each teacher. Use the Cambridge anchors to keep your language grounded.
4
On the KPI cadence — evaluate submissions
When teachers submit KPI evidence, review it in Teacher KPI Evaluation and confirm scores. Keep the Competency Framework open as your shared vocabulary.

These keep your evaluation fair and trusted by teachers.

You evaluate your school's teachers only. The KPI dashboard and appraisal forms filter to your school automatically. You will not see — and should not score — teachers from other schools.
Anchor your scoring, do not invent it. Every appraisal and KPI item links to a Cambridge Teacher Standard (the CTS chips) and the framework. Score against the anchor, not a gut feeling. Click the chip if you are unsure what the standard means.
Calibration is not a competition. Comparing your scores with peers is about fairness for teachers, not about who is "right". If you drift high or low, that is normal — calibration is how you bring it back in line.
You do not change roles or schools. Who is a teacher, which school they belong to, and account details are all managed by Eduversal from the Central Hub User Console — not from any page here.

Short, plain meanings for the terms that show up most often in your pages.

Appraisalformal review
The structured, scored review of a teacher against the network framework. Anchored to Cambridge Teacher Standards.
Walkthroughquick visit
A short, frequent classroom visit with light notes. Builds a fair picture over time — the evidence base for the formal appraisal.
KPIKey Performance Indicator
An agreed measure of a teacher's performance. Teachers submit evidence; you evaluate and confirm the score.
Calibrationscoring fairness
Comparing your scores with other appraisers so the same performance gets the same score everywhere. Also called inter-rater reliability.
Competencya skill
A specific professional skill we expect leaders to grow. Each competency has 4 stages: Awareness, Practitioner, Advanced, Lead.
Domaingroup of competencies
A group of related competencies. The Leadership framework has 6 domains; the Competency Framework page shows them all.
CTSCambridge Teacher Standards 2023
The Cambridge document the teacher appraisal + KPI items anchor to. Example: CTS 4.1. Click any chip to read the exact source text.
CSLSCambridge School Leader Standards 2023
The leadership-side Cambridge document the Competency Framework anchors to. Example: CSLS 4.1.
ESEduversal Academic Standards
Our 23-section network policy manual. Example: ES 7.3. Grounds appraisal and quality work.
schoolIdschool binding
A hidden field on your profile that says which school you support. It is what filters the KPI + appraisal pages to your teachers only. Set by Eduversal.
