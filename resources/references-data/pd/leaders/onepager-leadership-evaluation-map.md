# The Leadership Evaluation Map — for School Leaders

As a school leader, you sit in the middle of the evaluation system. You **evaluate your teachers**. And you **are evaluated** as a leader. This page maps both, so you know which system does what.

> **In one sentence:** you run three systems *for your teachers* (KPI, Appraisal, Competency), and you grow through your own *leader* systems (Leadership Competency + Principal Evaluation). They all sit on the Cambridge standards.

---

## Part 1 — What you evaluate (your teachers)

These are the systems you use *on your staff*. (For the full detail, see the teacher one-pagers.)

| System | What you do | Your role |
|---|---|---|
| **Teacher KPI** | Set 15 yearly targets with weights; check at mid-year and year-end. | You set weights + evaluate. |
| **Teacher Appraisal v2.1** | Observe one formal lesson (F2 = 50%), review work (F1, F3), share the student survey (F4). Score 1–4 → A–F. | You are the **appraiser**. |
| **Walkthroughs** | Short, informal classroom visits to support teaching (not formal appraisal). | You observe + coach. |
| **Teacher Competency** | Confirm a teacher's evidence when they claim a level. | You confirm; the teacher self-assesses first. |

> **The most important one to get right: F2.** It is half of every teacher's appraisal, and *you* score it. If your "3" is not the same as another leader's "3", appraisal is not fair. That is why observer calibration matters — see the calibration session + workbook.

---

## Part 2 — What evaluates you (as a leader)

These are the systems that support *your* growth as a leader.

| System | What it is | Scale |
|---|---|---|
| **Leadership Competency Framework** | Your long-term growth map as a leader — 6 domains. | 4-level ladder: Awareness → Practitioner → Advanced → Lead |
| **Principal Evaluation** (for principals) | A yearly leadership cycle: observation, annual appraisal, 360° feedback, and coaching with an Eduversal Director. | Per the principal frameworks |

**The 6 leadership competency domains** (`competency_framework/leaders`):

1. **EVSI** — Educational Vision & School Improvement
2. **CIAL** — Curriculum, Instruction & Assessment Leadership
3. **PDPC** — People Development & Professional Capacity
4. **EWSC** — Equity, Wellbeing & School Culture
5. **EAO** — Ethics, Accountability & Operations
6. **FCEP** — Family, Community & External Partnerships

Each domain points back to a **Cambridge School Leader Standard 2023** (CSLS) — the leadership version of the teacher standards.

> **Words used here:** **CSLS** = Cambridge School Leader Standards. **360°** = feedback gathered from people around you (those you lead, peers, and those you report to). **F2** = the class-observation part of the teacher appraisal.

## How it fits together

```
        YOU (school leader)
        │
        ├── evaluate your teachers ──►  KPI · Appraisal (F2!) · Walkthroughs · Competency
        │                                (teacher systems — you run these)
        │
        └── grow as a leader ───────►  Leadership Competency (6 domains)
                                         + Principal Evaluation (principals)
                                         (leader systems — these support you)

        Backbone for both:  Cambridge standards
        (CTS 2023 for teachers · CSLS 2023 for leaders)
```

---

## What this means for you

- **Two hats, one backbone.** You evaluate teachers against Cambridge Teacher Standards; you grow against Cambridge School Leader Standards. Same logic, different lens.
- **Fairness is your job.** When you score F2, you protect the fairness of the whole network. Calibrate with other leaders so the same lesson earns the same score everywhere.
- **You are supported, not just measured.** Your own growth has a map (Leadership Competency) and, for principals, a coaching relationship with an Eduversal Director.
- **The systems do not feed each other.** A teacher's KPI does not change their appraisal grade. Your evaluation of a teacher does not change your own leadership competency level. Keep them separate.
