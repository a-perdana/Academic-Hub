# Facilitator Session Guide — Your Leadership Competency (Guided Walkthrough)

> **Read `_facilitator-conventions.md` first** — especially §1 (ESL / EAL) and §2 (the five timing blocks).

---

## Why this session exists

School leaders spend their time growing *others*. Their own growth often goes unplanned. The Leadership Competency Framework gives them a map — but, like teachers, leaders opening it for the first time are unsure which level they are at and what counts as evidence.

This session is a guided first use for leaders. They learn the 6 domains, the 4-level ladder, and the evidence habit — then choose one leadership competency to grow. After this, they can use the Learning Path and My Portfolio pages alone, and model the same honest self-assessment they will ask of their teachers.

---

## What the leaders framework is (facilitator background)

The leaders track has **6 domains** and **29 competencies** (`competency_framework/leaders`, v3):

| Domain | What it covers |
|---|---|
| **EVSI** — Educational Vision & School Improvement | setting direction + driving improvement |
| **CIAL** — Curriculum, Instruction & Assessment Leadership | leading teaching + assessment quality |
| **PDPC** — People Development & Professional Capacity | growing your staff |
| **EWSC** — Equity, Wellbeing & School Culture | a fair, safe, healthy school |
| **EAO** — Ethics, Accountability & Operations | running the school well + with integrity |
| **FCEP** — Family, Community & External Partnerships | parents + community + the wider network |

Each competency uses the same **4-level ladder**: **Awareness → Practitioner → Advanced → Lead**. Each domain points back to a **Cambridge School Leader Standard 2023** (CSLS) — the leadership companion to the teacher standards. (**CSLS** = Cambridge School Leader Standards; **CPD** = Continuing Professional Development.)

---

## Learning objectives

By the end of this session, each leader can:

1. **Name** the 6 leadership domains and what each covers.
2. **Pick** an honest level for one leadership competency, using evidence.
3. **Explain** what evidence proves a leadership level (and what does not).
4. **Choose** one competency to grow and a first step.

## Materials & setup

- The one-pager `onepager-leadership-evaluation-map.md`.
- A device per leader, logged in to the Academic Hub (or a projected walkthrough).
- A simple self-assessment slip: *domain → competency → honest level → evidence*.

**Face-to-face:** if devices are limited, project the Learning Path and do one domain together.
**Online:** everyone on a device; facilitator demos, then breakout rooms for individual work.

> **Before the session:** confirm logins. Have the paper slip as a back-up if the platform is unavailable.

## Facilitator talking points

**On modelling.** *"You ask your teachers to look honestly at their own practice. Today you do the same thing with your leadership. When teachers see you self-assess honestly — including the levels where you are only at Awareness — they trust the system more."*

**On the ladder for leaders.** *"Awareness — I understand this leadership practice. Practitioner — I do it regularly in my school. Advanced — I do it well and adapt it to my context. Lead — I help other leaders do it, across schools or the network."* (`competency_framework/leaders` → 4-level ladder).

**On evidence (the heart of the session).** *"A leadership level is not a feeling about yourself. It is what you can point to: the improvement plan you drove, the staff member you developed, the culture change with data behind it. 'Advanced' is not 'I could lead this' — it is 'here is where I did, and here is what changed.'"*

**On the Cambridge backbone.** *"Each domain points back to a Cambridge School Leader Standard. So your growth map uses the same recognised standards as the rest of the Cambridge world — not one network's opinion."* (`cambridge/school-leader-standards-2023.json`).

## Activity instructions

### Self-assess three leadership competencies (24 min)
Each leader picks **3** competencies across different domains. For each:
1. Read the level descriptors on the Learning Path page.
2. Pick the honest level they are at **now**.
3. Write **one piece of real evidence**.

Rule: **no level without evidence.** No evidence → drop one level.

- **Online:** breakout rooms of 3; facilitator drops in to reinforce the evidence habit.

### Choose one to grow (12 min)
From the three, each leader picks **one** to move up one level, and writes one first step for the next month.

> **Why this works:** leaders leave with their own growth plan — and with first-hand experience of the honesty they will ask their teachers for.

## Common questions (answer in simple English)

**"Is my leadership competency the same as my principal appraisal?"**
No. Competency is your long-term growth map (you self-assess, over years). Principal appraisal is a yearly evaluation cycle. They are separate — see the evaluation map one-pager.

**"What if I am only at Awareness in a whole domain?"**
That is honest and normal. Leadership is wide. A clear map of where you are is far more useful than a map full of guesses.

**"Who confirms my evidence?"**
You self-assess first. A reviewer can confirm when you claim a level in your portfolio — the same way you confirm your teachers' evidence.

## Exit ticket

1. One leadership competency I now know my honest level in.
2. One I will grow, and my first step.
3. One thing I am still unsure about.

> Collect line 3 to see where leaders need clearer level descriptors or examples.
