# Facilitator Session Guide — Setting Teacher KPI Targets (for School Leaders)

> **Read `_facilitator-conventions.md` first** — especially §1 (ESL / EAL) and §2 (the five timing blocks).

---

## Why this session exists

School leaders set the **weights** for the 15 teacher KPIs and agree **targets** with each teacher. But two problems are common:

1. **Weights drift to "all equal".** When every KPI feels important, leaders weight them evenly — and the system stops signalling any priority at all.
2. **Targets become top-down numbers.** A target handed down without a conversation feels like pressure, not support — and teachers disengage.

This session helps leaders do both well: set weights that say something true about the school's year, and run a target conversation that a teacher leaves feeling supported, not judged.

---

## Learning objectives

By the end of this session, each leader can:

1. **Explain** how KPI weights signal the school's priorities.
2. **Set** a weight profile for their school that reflects a real priority — not "all equal".
3. **Run** a supportive target-setting conversation using open questions.
4. **Avoid** the common traps (unfair targets, targets outside a teacher's control).

---

## Materials & setup

- The one-pager `onepager-leadership-evaluation-map.md`.
- The teacher one-pager `../teachers/onepager-how-kpi-works.md` (so leaders see what teachers see).
- A printed "KPI weight profile" sheet listing the 15 KPIs (leaders mark a weight against each, total 100%).
- A "target conversation" role-play card (a sample teacher profile).

**Face-to-face:** tables grouped by school, so a school's leaders set their profile together.
**Online:** breakout rooms by school; a shared sheet per school for the weight profile.

## Facilitator talking points

**On weights as signals.** *"The 15 KPIs are network-wide, but YOU set the weights. The weight is a message to your teachers: 'this is what our school is focusing on this year.' If you weight everything equally, you send no message. Pick a real priority and let the weights show it."* (`teacher-kpi-v1.json → totalWeight = 100`).

**On the leadership standard behind this.** *"Setting clear priorities and developing your people is core school-leader work. This is not admin — it is leadership."* (Cambridge School Leader Standards 2023 — people development + school improvement; `cambridge/school-leader-standards-2023.json`).

**On the target conversation (the heart of Activity 2).** A target should be **agreed**, not assigned. Use open questions:
- *"Looking at this KPI, where do you think you are now?"*
- *"What would a realistic step forward look like this year?"*
- *"What support would help you reach it?"*
- *"What gets in the way — and what is in your control?"*

*"When a teacher helps choose the target, they own it. When you hand it down, they comply at best."*

**On the traps:**
1. **Targets outside the teacher's control.** "All students score 90%" is not in the teacher's control. "Run a weekly retrieval quiz and track it" is. Score the action, support the outcome.
2. **Same target for everyone.** A new teacher and a lead teacher are not at the same point. Targets should stretch each person fairly, from where they are.
3. **No support attached.** A target without support is just pressure. Every target should come with one named support.

## Activity instructions

### Activity 1 — Set your school's weight profile (25 min)
Each school's leaders work together. They distribute weights across the 15 KPIs to total **100%**, so the profile shows **one clear priority** for the year. They must be able to answer: *"What does this profile tell a teacher to focus on first?"*

> Constraint: they may not weight everything equally. The point is to make a choice.

- **Online:** one shared sheet per school in a breakout room.

### Activity 2 — Role-play the target conversation (26 min)
In pairs, one plays the leader, one plays the teacher (use the role-play card). They run a target-setting conversation for **one** KPI, using the open questions. Then swap roles. Each gives the other one piece of Glow/Grow/Go feedback on *how the conversation felt*.

- **Online:** breakout rooms of 2; facilitator drops in to listen.

> **Why this works:** leaders practise the actual conversation, not just the theory. They feel the difference between "assigned" and "agreed" from both sides of the table.

## Common questions (answer in simple English)

**"Can every school have a different weight profile?"**
Yes. The 15 KPIs are the same network-wide, but each school sets its own weights to match its priorities this year.

**"What if a teacher disagrees with a target?"**
Good — that is a conversation, not a conflict. Ask what *they* think is realistic and why. A target you both believe in works far better than one you imposed.

**"How often do we revisit targets?"**
At the mid-year check. The mid-year point exists so you can adjust together if something is off track.

**"Is the KPI conversation the same as appraisal?"**
No. KPI is about agreeing yearly targets (results). Appraisal is your formal observation of how they teach. Keep the conversations separate so neither one contaminates the other.

## Exit ticket

1. One change I will make to how I set KPI weights.
2. One thing I will do differently in a target conversation.
3. One thing I am still unsure about.

> Collect line 3 — it tells you (and Eduversal) where leaders need more support on the KPI process.
