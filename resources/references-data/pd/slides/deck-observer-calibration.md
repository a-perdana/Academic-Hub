# Slide Deck Outline — Observer Calibration (F2)

> One `## Slide N` = one slide. Bullets = on-screen. Speaker note = what you say. ESL/EAL: short bullets, gloss terms.

## Slide 1 — Title
- **Observer Calibration**
- Scoring F2 the same way, every time
- For school leaders

**Speaker note:** "Today we make sure a '3' in your school means the same as a '3' everywhere."

## Slide 2 — The fairness problem
- Same lesson · two observers · different scores.
- That is not fair to the teacher.
- Calibration fixes it.

**Speaker note:** Run "True for me": "I have been unsure of a score." Hands up.

## Slide 3 — Why F2 matters most
- F2 = **50%** of the appraisal.
- A **person** scores it.
- So the person must be calibrated.

**Speaker note:** "Half the grade depends on your judgement. Let us make that judgement consistent."
**Visual:** pie chart, F2 half.

## Slide 4 — Score evidence, not impression
- The rubric asks: **what happened?**
- Not: "is this a good teacher?"
- Score what you **saw and heard**.

**Speaker note:** "Not reputation. Not how hard they tried. Evidence."

## Slide 5 — Read the rubric closely: 3 vs 4
- Level 3: "minor pacing gaps… do not undermine coherence"
- Level 4: "transitions **explicitly planned**"
- One phrase decides it.

**Speaker note:** Show how one word separates the levels.
**Visual:** two rubric levels, key phrase highlighted.

## Slide 6 — Score alone (Activity 1)
- Score the vignette · 3 items · **silently, alone**.
- Write one line of evidence per score.
- No talking yet.

**Speaker note:** Silence stops people copying each other.

## Slide 7 — Reveal
- Show all scores at once.
- See the spread.

**Speaker note:** Use a poll (online) or hands (in person).
**Visual:** a simple spread chart.

## Slide 8 — Reconcile (Activity 2)
- Do **not** average to 3.5.
- Go back to the **rubric**.
- Find the one word you read differently.

**Speaker note:** "Reconcile by evidence, not by splitting the difference."

## Slide 9 — Three traps
- **Halo** — high scores everywhere for a teacher you rate
- **Intent** — scoring what they meant, not what happened
- **Loose reading** — treating level-3 words as level-4

**Speaker note:** Name each trap in the debrief.

## Slide 10 — Second vignette (borderline)
- Score the hard case.
- Did the spread get smaller?

**Speaker note:** "If the spread narrowed, calibration is working."

## Slide 11 — Close
- We score **evidence against the rubric**.
- Same lesson → same score, everywhere.
- Calibrate before every appraisal season.

**Speaker note:** Exit ticket — collect "the F2 item I am still unsure about".
