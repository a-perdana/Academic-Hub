Core Services

What Eduversal Provides

Each service is designed to raise academic standards, ensure Cambridge compliance, and support the sustainable growth of partner schools across Indonesia.

Assessment

Centralised Assessment

The Eduversal Academic Standardised Examination (EASE) is a centralised assessment administered to all partner schools four times per academic year. It provides a network-wide benchmark, enabling schools to track student performance against a shared standard and identify areas for targeted intervention.

  • Four assessment cycles per year (EASE I, II, III, and A-EASE I for A-Level)
  • Covers key Cambridge subject areas across IGCSE and AS/A-Level programmes
  • Results are visible through the Academic Hub dashboard with school-level and subject-level breakdowns
  • Historical archive available for year-on-year trend analysis
  • Analytics tools for identifying high-performing and at-risk cohorts
School Visit

Appraisal Visits

Eduversal Subject Specialists conduct structured appraisal visits to partner schools one to two times per academic year. These visits combine classroom observation, teacher walkthrough sessions, and structured interviews with school leadership to produce a detailed report aligned to the Eduversal Appraisal Framework.

  • Specialist-led visits by trained Subject Specialists in Math, English, Sciences, and Bahasa
  • Structured classroom walkthrough observations with standardised rubrics
  • Appraiser calibration sessions to ensure consistency across schools
  • Post-visit reports with actionable recommendations and follow-up targets
  • Full digital record of all observations accessible in Academic Hub
Cambridge

Cambridge Centre Compliance

Eduversal supports partner schools in meeting and maintaining their obligations as Cambridge International Centres. This includes examination entry management, results review, pathway planning, and preparation for Cambridge school quality reviews.

  • Guidance on Cambridge Centre requirements and annual compliance obligations
  • Cambridge Pathway Simulator to model subject combinations and predicted grades
  • Cambridge Exam dashboard tracking entries, results, and grade distributions
  • Cambridge Calendar integration for key examination and registration deadlines
  • Support with Cambridge School Quality and accreditation processes (CIS/WASC)
Quality Assurance

School Quality & KPI Monitoring

Eduversal provides a comprehensive school quality assurance framework, combining externally-set KPIs with school self-appraisal tools. This allows partner schools to benchmark their performance across academic, operational, and community dimensions, while supporting accreditation readiness for CIS and WASC.

  • School Performance KPI dashboard with tracking across multiple quality domains
  • Annual School Self-Appraisal tool for structured internal review
  • School Appraisals Dashboard aggregating all visit outcomes by school
  • Network-wide audit view identifying patterns across the partner school network
  • Accreditation Toolkit aligned to CIS and WASC self-study requirements
  • Partner Schools Performance and Islamic Schools Performance dashboards
Professional Development

Teacher Appraisal & Development

Beyond school-level appraisal, Eduversal provides a structured system for teacher appraisal and professional growth. School leaders can record classroom observations, submit appraisal entries, and track individual teacher development over time — all within Academic Hub.

  • Teacher Appraisal Entry forms for structured post-observation documentation
  • Classroom Walkthrough Entry tool with subject-specific observation criteria
  • My Observations log for appraisers to track their own walkthrough history
  • Teacher KPI Evaluation framework aligned to national and Cambridge teaching standards
  • Appraiser Calibration sessions to standardise observation quality across schools
  • Coordinator Competency Framework and Learning Path for academic coordinators
Safeguarding

Safeguarding & Child Protection

Eduversal requires all partner schools to maintain a robust safeguarding and child protection policy in line with both Indonesian law and Cambridge International requirements. This is not optional — it is a core compliance obligation for all schools in the network, and a dedicated section of the Academic Standards framework.

  • Dedicated Section 09 in the Eduversal Academic Standards covering safeguarding policy, procedures, and evidence requirements
  • Child protection policy template aligned to Cambridge Centre compliance requirements
  • Student wellbeing frameworks covering mental health, inclusion, and pastoral care
  • Clear reporting lines and designated safeguarding lead (DSL) role requirements
  • Data protection obligations for safeguarding records (Section 14: Data Protection)
  • Risk management protocols for safeguarding incidents (Section 15: Risk Management)
  • Staff recruitment safeguarding checks integrated into the Staff Lifecycle framework (Section 12)

Curriculum

Curriculum Guidance & Learning Pathway

Eduversal supports partner schools in mapping their curriculum to Cambridge requirements and national standards. Schools have access to curriculum planning tools, subject pacing guides, and an academic calendar — enabling consistent, standards-aligned delivery across the network.

  • Curriculum Map aligned to Cambridge IGCSE, AS/A-Level, and Checkpoint frameworks
  • Academic Calendar with key Eduversal and Cambridge milestones for the school year
  • Subject pacing guides (via Teachers Hub) for Math, Sciences, and English at all levels
  • Inclusion, EAL, and Learning Support framework (Section 08 of Academic Standards)
  • Student Conduct and Discipline standards with restorative practice guidance (Section 22)
  • External Partnerships and Student Pathways support for university and career guidance (Section 23)
Academic Standards Framework

A 23-Section Framework Covering Every Dimension of School Quality

The Eduversal Academic Standards is a comprehensive policy and operational framework developed for partner schools in the Cambridge network across Indonesia. It covers governance, legal compliance, teaching quality, student wellbeing, safeguarding, examinations, operations, and external relations — in a single structured document available to all partner school leaders.

Each section is maintained as a living document in Google Docs and accessible through the Academic Standards portal. School leaders with an Academic Hub account can access the full interactive version with inline AI assistance.

How It Works

Becoming a Partner School

Eduversal's services are available exclusively to schools that have formally joined the partner network.

1
Join the Network

Your school enters a formal partnership agreement with Eduversal (Yayasan Edukasi Universal Indonesia). This establishes the school as an active Cambridge-aligned partner within the Eduversal network and unlocks access to all academic services.

2
Access Academic Hub

School leadership — Foundation Representatives, Principals, Academic Coordinators, and Cambridge Exam Officers — are onboarded to the Academic Hub portal. After account creation and admin approval, each role sees a tailored dashboard with tools relevant to their responsibilities.

3
Data-Driven Progress

With EASE results, appraisal visit data, KPI tracking, and the Academic Standards framework all in one place, your school can make evidence-based decisions, track improvement over time, and ensure full compliance with Cambridge and Eduversal requirements year-round.

Partnership Commitments

What We Expect from Partner Schools

A genuine partnership means mutual accountability. These are the core commitments Eduversal asks of every partner school.

Safeguarding Policy

Every partner school must maintain an up-to-date safeguarding and child protection policy with a designated lead, clear reporting procedures, and staff trained to recognise and respond to concerns. This is a non-negotiable compliance requirement under both Indonesian law and Cambridge Centre obligations.

EASE Participation

All partner schools are expected to participate in all four EASE cycles each year, submitting student data on time and using the results to inform teaching and intervention planning across subject areas.

Cooperation with Appraisal Visits

Partner schools must cooperate fully with Eduversal appraisal visits — providing classroom access, making teachers available for observation, and engaging honestly with post-visit feedback and action plans.

Cambridge Centre Obligations

Schools are responsible for maintaining their Cambridge Centre registration, ensuring exam entry deadlines are met, and implementing Cambridge's policies on assessment integrity, student conduct, and centre operations.

Academic Standards Alignment

Partner schools are expected to work towards alignment with all 23 sections of the Eduversal Academic Standards framework over time, with priority given to governance, safeguarding, teaching quality, and Cambridge compliance.

Data & Reporting

Schools must maintain accurate student data, submit self-appraisal reports as required, and use Academic Hub tools as the primary record system for academic performance, teacher appraisal, and school quality monitoring.
