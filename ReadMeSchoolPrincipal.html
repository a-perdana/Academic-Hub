As a School Principal, you have a group of pages in the Admin → For School Principal menu. They cover how your school is doing, your own coaching and development, your school's AI readiness, and the students enrolling into the Students Hub. This page explains each one in plain English. New words are in Section 7 — Glossary.

A short promise
You do not need to learn all of this today. Every page here shows your school's data only — never another school's. Read this once so you know which page answers which question, then use them when the moment comes.
🏫
What is your scope?
Almost every page in Academic Hub filters to your school. The KPI scorecard, appraisals, EASE results, student roster, assessment roll-up — all show your school only. Other Principals see their own schools, never yours.
🧭
What are these pages for?
Three things: school-level review (self-appraisal + KPI + your own coaching), AI readiness (validating teacher AI use + your school's AI maturity), and the Students Hub bridge (approving students + watching assessment results).
Where to find these pages. They live under Admin → For School Principal in the navbar. You see this menu because you hold the School Principal role. If a page redirects you with ?denied=... in the address, that is a role gate — tell Eduversal.

How they connect. The leadership group is about you and your school's targets. The AI group is about getting your school ready for AI in learning. The Students Hub group is about the students themselves — letting them in and watching how they do. You will dip into each group at different times of the year.

Each card tells you four things: what the page is, why we built it, what you will see, and what to do.

📝
School Appraisal
/school-self-appraisal
Open page →
What it is
A self-appraisal of your whole school against Eduversal's quality standards. You rate where your school stands and write short evidence notes.
Why we built it
Honest self-review is the first step to improvement. It also gives Eduversal a starting picture before a school-quality visit, so the visit is a conversation, not a surprise. Anchored to Eduversal Academic Standards (ES).
What you see
A structured form covering school-quality areas, with space to record evidence for each.
What to do
Complete it honestly with your leadership team. A fair self-picture helps more than an over-confident one.
📊
School KPI Entry
/school-performance-kpi
Open page →
What it is
Where you record your school's key performance indicators — the agreed numbers that show how the school is doing across the year.
Why we built it
So school performance is tracked the same way across the network, and so you and Eduversal look at the same scorecard. It turns "I think we are doing well" into "here is the evidence".
What you see
A scorecard of KPI rows for your school. You enter or update the values for the current period.
What to do
Update it on the cadence Eduversal agrees with you (often termly). Keep it current — stale numbers make the scorecard useless.
💬
Principal Coaching
/principal-coaching-view
Open page →
What it is
A read-only view of your own coaching sessions — the notes your Eduversal Director mentor logs after each coaching conversation with you.
Why we built it
So you can revisit what you and your mentor agreed, see your growth over time, and prepare for the next session. Coaching is support, not judgement.
What you see
Your coaching session records. A "personal check-in" stage may be marked private.
What to do
Read before each new session. This page is confidential to you and your mentor.
  • Your Foundation Rep cannot see this — coaching is a safe space, separate from appraisal.
  • Only your Eduversal Director mentor writes here; you read.
🛡️
AI Validity Assessment
/ai-validate-teacher-assessments
Open page →
What it is
A review queue where you, as line manager, check the AI Competency self-assessments your teachers have submitted, and agree or adjust the level they claimed.
Why we built it
Teachers rate their own AI skills first. A second pair of eyes — yours — keeps the ratings fair and consistent. This is the AI Competency Framework Part 1 validation step.
What you see
A list of your school's teacher self-assessments waiting for review. For each, you confirm the level and can leave a manager note.
What to do
Work through the queue when teachers submit. Approve the agreed level, or note where you see it differently — kindly and clearly.
📈
AI Maturity Assessment
/ai-maturity-self-assessment
Open page →
What it is
Your school's once-a-year self-assessment of AI maturity — 5 levels across 6 areas. It asks "how ready is our whole school for AI in teaching and learning?"
Why we built it
AI in schools is new. This gives every school a shared map of where it is and where to grow next. It is Part 3 of the AI Competency Framework — the institution-level view.
What you see
A grid of 6 areas × 5 maturity levels. You (the Principal) edit; your AC and FR can read.
What to do
Complete it once a year with your leadership team.
  • After you submit, the ratings lock until the Eduversal Academic Directorate visit validates them — so submit when you are ready.
🧑‍🎓
Student Roster
/student-roster
Open page →
What it is
Your school's Students Hub roster. Students sign into the Students Hub, pick your school's class, and wait for approval. Here you review them and decide.
Why we built it
Only real students at your school should reach the Students Hub. This page is the gate — you approve genuine students, graduate leavers, and remove duplicates or mistakes.
What you see
A filterable list (by status + grade) with actions: Approve, Graduate, Remove, Reactivate. Shows your school's students only.
What to do
Check the "pending approval" list regularly during enrolment windows. Approving turns a waiting student into an active one. The Academic Coordinator can help with this too.
🧩
School Assessment
/school-assessment
Open page →
What it is
A school-wide roll-up of chapter-test results across your Year 7–8 classes — average scores, pass rates, and how many students need help.
Why we built it
So you can see, at a glance, where your school is strong and where a class or subject needs support — without opening each class one by one.
What you see
Per-class cards with average, pass rate, and intervention count. Subject + grade filters. Read-only — the data flows up from teachers' chapter tests.
What to do
Review it after each chapter-test round. Use the intervention flags to start a conversation with the Academic Coordinator and the teachers.

This is the single most important idea behind your pages. Once you understand it, everything else makes sense.

1
Your account is tied to one school
Eduversal links your profile to your partner school. You cannot change this — it is set by Eduversal so the data filter is always correct.
2
Every page filters to that school automatically
KPI, appraisals, student roster, assessment roll-up — they all quietly show your school and hide every other school. You do not need to pick a school each time.
3
Other leaders see their own schools
A Principal at another school sees their data, not yours. Your Academic Coordinator and Foundation Rep share your school's view because they belong to the same school.
4
If a page looks empty, check the filters first
Sometimes a page is empty because a grade or subject filter is set, or because no data has been entered yet — not because it is broken. Clear filters, then ask Eduversal if it still looks wrong.

These keep your school's data safe and your coaching private.

Your coaching is private. Only you and your Eduversal Director mentor can see your coaching record. Your Foundation Rep is excluded by design, so coaching stays a safe space — separate from the appraisal that decides your grade.
AI Maturity locks after submit. Once you submit the yearly AI maturity self-assessment, the ratings lock until the Eduversal Academic Directorate visit validates them. Agree your answers with your team before submitting.
Approving a student is a real action. When you approve a student on the roster, they get full access to the Students Hub. Approve genuine students only, and graduate or remove the rest, so the roster stays clean and trustworthy.
You do not change roles or schools. Who is a teacher, who is a student, which school anyone belongs to — all managed by Eduversal from the Central Hub User Console, not from any page here.

Short, plain meanings for the terms that show up most often in your pages.

KPIKey Performance Indicator
An agreed number that shows how the school is doing in one area — e.g. attendance, pass rate, parent satisfaction. Tracked the same way across the network.
Self-appraisalschool-level
Your own honest review of the whole school against Eduversal quality standards — a starting picture before a school-quality visit.
Coachingsupport, not judgement
Regular development conversations with your Eduversal Director mentor. Confidential between you and your mentor — your Foundation Rep cannot see it.
AI ValidityPart 1 validation
Checking and agreeing the AI-skill level your teachers gave themselves. You are the line-manager second opinion.
AI MaturityPart 3, school-level
Your whole school's readiness for AI in learning — 5 levels × 6 areas, once a year. Locks after submit until validated.
Students Hubstudent portal
The separate Eduversal portal for students (Grade 7–8 pilot). Students enrol there; you approve them on the Student Roster.
Chapter testunit mastery
A short, network-uniform test per teaching unit, taken in the Students Hub. Results roll up into your School Assessment page.
Interventionflag for help
A signal that a student or class scored low enough to need extra support. The School Assessment page counts these per class.
ESEduversal Academic Standards
Our 23-section network policy manual. Example: ES 7.3. Grounds school-quality and appraisal work.
schoolIdschool binding
A hidden field on your profile that says which school you lead. It is what makes every page show your school only. Set by Eduversal.
